# Analysis of Food Commodity Prices in Nigeria (2002 - 2023)

## Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive visual analysis of food security and price trends in Nigeria. Using data from the **World Food Programme (WFP)**, the study explores how the prices of essential commodities like maize, rice, and beans have fluctuated over the past two decades.

## The Dataset
The dataset contains **80,981** records of market prices.
- **Timeframe:** 2002 – 2023
- **Scope:** 42 unique commodities across various Nigerian states.
- **Categories included:** cereals and tubers, pulses and nuts, non-food, oil and fats, meat, fish and eggs, milk and dairy, vegetables and fruits, miscellaneous food.

## Visualizations Included
To understand the data, this repository includes several Matplotlib-based visualizations:
1. **Bar Charts:** Comparing the average prices of the most expensive commodities.
2. **Scatter Plots:** Tracking the price movement of specific items (like Maize) over time.
3. **Histograms:** Showing the frequency distribution of price points across the dataset.
4. **Box Plots:** Analyzing price volatility and identifying outliers within food categories.
5. **Heatmaps:** Visualizing yearly price trends to identify periods of high inflation.

## Key Insights
- Identification of the most expensive food categories in the Nigerian market.
- Historical price spikes correlated with specific years.
- Price distribution patterns across different regions and markets.

## Technologies Used
- **Python**
- **Pandas** (Data Manipulation)
